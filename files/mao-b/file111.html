    Abstract

    Neurodegenerative diseases represent a challenge for biomedical research due to their high prevalence and lack of mechanism-based treatments. Because of the complex pathology of neurodegenerative disorders, multifunctional drugs have been increasingly recognized as potential treatments. We identified homobivalent γ-carbolinium salts as potent inihitors of both cholinesterases, N-methyl-d-aspartate receptors, and monoamine oxidases. Homobivalent γ-carbolines displayed similar structure–activity relationships on all tested targets and may present promising designed multiple ligands for the treatment of neurodegenerative disorders.

    Introduction

    ARTICLE SECTIONS
    Jump To
    Neurodegenerative disorders such as Alzheimer’s disease (AD) and Parkinson’s disease (PD) pose major challenges to 21st century health systems. Because of their complex pathology, neurodegenerative disorders are unlikely to be mitigated by drugs that address only one clinically relevant target, and the requirement for the development of multifunctional drugs has been increasingly recognized. Several potential drug targets for the treatment of cognition and memory defects in AD patients or the extrapyramidal symptoms in PD patients have been identified. For example, acetylcholinesterase (AChE) and butyrylcholinesterase (BChE) degrade acetylcholine in the synaptic clefts of cholinergic neurons. The loss of cholinergic neurotransmission in the frontal cortex and hippocampus is a hallmark of AD, and AChE inhibitors that elevate acetylcholine levels, such as donepezil, have become first-line therapeutics to slow down AD progression.(1) Glutamate mediates excitatory synaptic transmission in the brain, which is important for synaptic plasticity involved in long-term potentiation, memory, and learning.(2) Pathophysiological conditions resulting in high extracellular concentrations of glutamate favor the excessive activation of N-methyl-d-aspartate receptors (NR), which leads to an overload of cells with calcium that eventually causes the loss of neurons.(3-5) This process of NR-mediated, glutamate-induced excitotoxicity is effectively blocked by memantine, the only approved drug for the treatment of patients suffering from moderate to severe AD.(6) Monoamine oxidase (MAO) catalyzes the oxidative deamination of a variety of biogenic amines.(7) Two types of MAOs that are encoded by different genes, show different tissue distribution, and display different substrate and inhibitor specificities. MAO-A inhibitors were the first antidepressant drugs developed, whereas MAO-B inhibitors with neuroprotective properties have been found useful for the therapeutic management of PD.(8-10)
    The concept of designed multiple ligands (DMLs), in which distinct pharmacophores of different drugs are combined in the same molecule, is particularly attractive for the treatment of disorders involving multiple pathophysiological processes.(11-13) If bifunctional (heterobivalent) molecules are designed in which each pharmacophore binds to different clinically relevant targets, it may be assumed that this single-molecule combination therapy generates additive or synergistic effects that improve therapeutic efficacy. Another approach to creating DMLs would be the use of homobivalent molecules in which each half of the hybrid has identical binding properties but at multiple targets that are not accessible for their monovalent analogues.(14) Here, we report the synthesis and evaluation of homobivalent γ-carbolines as potential DMLs that address three different targets relevant to the treatment of neurodegenerative disorders: cholinesterases, NR, and MAO.

    Results and Discussion

    ARTICLE SECTIONS
    Jump To
    In this study, we investigated new homobivalent γ-carbolines as potential drugs for the treatment of neurodegenerative disorders. For convenience, we will use the term “indolo-N” to assign the positions of linkers that connect the carboline moieties in homobivalent structures. Indolo-N linker positions refer to N9 in β-carbolines and N5 in γ-carbolines, as outlined in Figure 1. The term “pyrido-N” will be used to address β-carbolines or γ-carbolines carrying alkyl groups at N2.
    Recently described bivalent β-carbolines(14) served as lead structures for our investigation. The bivalent β-carboline 1 was pyrido-N-methylated to give the quaternary salt 2, which was then hydrogenated to generate the 1,2,3,4-tetrahydro derivative 3 (Figure 1). We synthesized 18 new, bivalent γ-carboline derivatives and focused on compounds with 6–9 carbon linkers. According to general procedure 1 (see Experimental Section), two identical γ-carboline (5H-pyrido[4,3-b]indole) moieties (Figure 1) were connected at the indolo-N by an alkyl chain using alkyl halides to create a set of bivalent substances that differed in the length of the connecting alkyl chain, which ranged from 6 to 9 carbon atoms (series 4a–d). In addition, the effect of a methoxy group attached to the γ-carboline scaffold was investigated (4e). These substances were also treated according to general procedures 2 and 3 (see Experimental Section) to obtain quaternary salts (series 5a–h) and partially reduced compounds (series 6a–e) (Figure 1).
    Considering the most active bivalent β-carboline with a nonylene linker(14) as lead structure for further investigations, we were able to compare the properties of an analogous set of bivalent β-carbolines and γ-carbolines consisting of pyridine-unmethylated (1 vs 4d), pyrido-N-methylated (2 vs 5d), and N-methylated 1,2,3,4-tetrahydro derivatives (3 vs 6d) in parallel pharmacological testing. All compounds were characterized as DMLs for the three structures relevant to the treatment of neurodegenerative diseases: cholinesterases AChE and BChE, NMDA receptors of the composition NR1-1a/NR2A or NR1-1a/NR2B, and monoamine oxidases MAO-A and MAO-B.     Homobivalent β-carbolines are nanomolar cholinesterase inhibitors with a preference for AChE over BChE.(14) For example, the β-carbolinium salt 2 displayed IC50 values for AChE and BChE of 0.52 and 5.3 nM, respectively (Table 1). The potency of the bivalent β-carbolinium as an AChE inhibitor of cholinesterase was substantially decreased upon removal of the permanent positive charge (compound 1; IC50 = 753.2 nM) or the hydrogenation of the pyridine ring, as in 3 (IC50 = 17.3 nM). Homobivalent γ-carbolines displayed some interesting differences to the β-carboline analogues (Table 1). The bivalent quaternary γ-carboline with the nonylene linker (5d) showed similar AChE-inhibitory activity compared with the β-carbolinium 2, yet it showed an approximately 34-fold specificity for AChE over BChE (IC50 values 0.45 versus 15.7 nM). The unmethylated pyridyl derivative 4d served as a moderate inhibitor of AChE (IC50 = 40.0 nM). Although 4d was approximately 39-fold more active as an AChE inhibitor compared with its β-carboline analogue, it was still 100-fold less active than the quaternary γ-carboline 5d (Table 1). On the other hand, the N-methylated tetrahydro γ-carboline 6d and its β-carboline counterpart 3 were similarly active as AChE inhibitors, yet they were ∼37-fold less active than the γ-carbolinium 5d.
    In summary, homobivalent γ-carbolinium molecules with linker lengths of 7–9 carbons (5b–d) were the most potent cholinesterase inhibitors, with activities similar to the previously described β-carbolinium analogue 2. Both the removal of the permanent charge from the pyrido-N and the hydrogenation of the pyridine ring significantly reduced cholinesterase inhibition, as did extending the pyrido-N-linked alkyl chain (5f–h) or attachment of a methoxy group at position C6 (5e) (Table 1).
    Several studies have evaluated the potencies of homo- and heterodimeric AChE inhibitors.(15-17) A recurrent observation in these studies was that the monomeric compounds were less active than their bifunctional analogues. For example, a homobivalent tacrine with a heptylene tether, bis(7)-tacrine, was shown to exhibit a more than 1000-fold higher potency as an AChE inhibitor than monomeric tacrine.(16) Several other studies have asserted that pharmacophores should be connected with 7–9 methylene groups to produce the highest inhibitory potency against AChE.(16, 18) These observations are best explained by the dual binding of the pharmacophores to the catalytic center and the peripheral binding site of AChE.(19) Consistent with the literature data, we found that the monovalent pyrido-N-methylated γ-carbolinium is a weak inhibitor of AChE and BChE,(20) whereas its bivalent counterpart 5d displayed inhibitory activity against both enzymes in the low nanomolar range (Table 1). We consistently found that carbolines connected by 7–9 methylene groups were the most potent cholinesterase inhibitors. The use of shorter linkers produced less effective compounds, whereas longer linkers yielded highly potent compounds but also produced severe solubility problems.
    As reported previously,(14) the quaternary homobivalent β-carboline 2 inhibited glutamate-induced excitotoxicity in transgenic cell lines expressing two subtypes of NR with IC50 values in the range of the therapeutic gold standard memantine (Table 1). When we evaluated the new series of homobivalent γ-carbolines, we found that the quaternary homobivalent γ-carbolines 5c and 5d were the most active structures; they were approximately 3.5-fold more active than the corresponding β-carboline 2 and inhibited glutamate-induced excitotoxicity in the submicromolar range (5d: IC50 = 0.40 and 0.78 μM for NR1-1a/NR2A and NR1-1a/NR2B receptors, respectively; Table 1). The bivalent γ-carbolines showed similar structure–activity relationships in the cell-based NR inhibition assay as the β-carbolines; only the permanently charged molecules were active (5a–d), whereas the unmethylated and pyridine-hydrogenated derivatives were inactive (Table 1). Extending the pyrido-N-linked alkyl chain in the γ-carbolinium structure (5f–h) yielded no further improvement.
    We assumed that the observed inhibition of glutamate-induced excitotoxicity by the quaternary bivalent carbolines in the transgenic cell lines resulted from the blockage of calcium currents through ligand-gated NRs that would otherwise cause cell damage. To confirm this hypothesis with a complementary method, we performed electrophysiological measurements using the patch-clamp method on TsA201 cells expressing the NR1-1a/NR2A subtype.(21) We tested the most active bivalent γ-carboline 5d and its unmethylated derivative 4d at a concentration of 10 μM in comparison with the reference inhibitor memantine. Compound 5d almost completely blocked NR1-1a/NR2A at 10 μM, i.e., 25-fold above the IC50 determined in the cell-based excitotoxicity assay. In contrast, compound 4d had no effect at the same concentration (Figure 2). The NR-blocking effect of 5d was observed upon preincubation of cells with the compound before the addition of agonists and did not depend on the membrane potential (data not shown), suggesting that 5d modulates the NR by binding to an allosteric site rather than in the ion channel.
    Recently, the homobivalent AChE inhibitor bis(7)-tacrine(16) has been shown to act as an antagonist of glutamate-induced excitotoxicity in preparations of rat cerebellar granule neurons.(22) In our hands, monomeric tacrine inhibited glutamate-induced excitotoxicity in transgenic cells expressing the NR2A subtype with a potency similar to memantine (see Table 1). Although we have not tested bivalent tacrine analogues, it seems that the NR inhibitory potency of this compound is mainly attributed to the activity of the monomeric tacrine. This is an interesting observation considering that, unlike tacrine, monomeric carbolines either failed to block NRs or displayed only weak antagonistic effects toward NR,(14, 20) whereas the bivalent carbolinium displayed high activity.
    The hallucinogenic β-carboline harmine has a distinct binding site in the active center cavity of MAO-A but does not bind efficiently to MAO-B.(23) Although the carbolines evaluated in this study lacked the harmine-like O-alkyl side chain that greatly increases the affinity of harmine for MAO-A,(23) it was tempting to evaluate whether the γ-carbolines served as inhibitors of MAO-A and/or MAO-B and probably provide an additional benefit as agents against neurodegenerative disorders. The monomeric pharmacophores γ-carboline(20) and 2-methyl-γ-carboline(20) as the precursors of 4 and 5 were determined to inhibit MAO-A with IC50 values of 3.3 ± 0.1 and 4.0 ± 1.4 μM, respectively. These compounds displayed a moderate selectivity for MAO-A and inhibited MAO-B with IC50 values 272.4 ± 24.7 μM (γ-carboline) and 201.4 ± 20.0 μM (2-methyl-γ-carboline). The bivalent γ-carbolines 4 displayed similar inhibitory activities against MAO-A and MAO-B, with IC50 values in the micromolar range and almost independent of linker length (Table 1). The pyrido-N-methylated compounds 5 showed a more than 10-fold higher affinity for both MAO-A and MAO-B compared the unmethylated analogues 4, with IC50 values in the submicromolar range (Table 1). The inhibitory potencies of these compounds were similar between linker lengths of 7–9 methylene groups, suggesting an unusual high-affinity mode of binding.
    Binda et al.(24) proposed a bipartite active site model for MAO-B and suggested that substrates or inhibitors may at first bind to a “substrate entrance cavity” before they cross the approximately 20 Å distance to the “substrate cavity” near the flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD) prosthetic group. We performed docking studies in MAO-B to analyze the possible binding mode of the homobivalent γ-carbolinium derivatives. The docking results of 5d in human MAO-B clearly support the assumption that the high affinity of 5d can be attributed to its binding into both the substrate entrance and substrate cavity at the same time (Supporting Information (SI), Figures S1–S3).
    The homobivalent γ-carbolinium 5d may present an interesting new function in addition to its cholinesterase- and NR-blocking activities. However, irreversible, nonspecific MAO inhibitors are infamous for causing the “cheese effect”, i.e., provoking adverse interactions with foods and beverages containing high amounts of tyramine.(25) Although our docking studies imply a noncovalent and competitive binding mode for the bivalent γ-carbolines, the nanomolar IC50 values reported in Table 1 may require further development of the compounds into either MAO-A- or MAO-B-specific inhibitors.
    The cholinergic system plays an important role in cognitive function. Scopolamine blocks central muscarinic acetylcholine receptors and induces transient cognitive deficits that are similar to the cognitive impairments seen in AD patients.(26) The eight-arm radial maze is a suitable assay to evaluate drugs for their ability to antagonize central scopolamine-induced memory defects.(26, 27) Here, we used the eight-arm radial maze assay to determine whether the most potent bivalent carbolinium salts 2 and 5d may be able to enter the CNS and antagonize scopolamine-induced memory defects by means of their potent cholinesterase inhibitory activities. Intraperitoneal administration of scopolamine to trained animals caused a significant delay in the total run time and a substantial increase in the number of errors (SI, Figure S4). The administration of either compound 2 or 5d 10 min after the administration of scopolamine had a minor, nonsignificant effect on the scopolamine-induced deceleration of the animals, yet the number of errors was greatly reduced (SI, Figure S4). Considering that the observed effects are linked to the enhancement of cholinergic neurotransmission by the compounds due their potent AChE-inhibitory activity, we conclude that the molecules may cross the blood–brain barrier and exert central effects.

    Conclusions

    ARTICLE SECTIONS
    Jump To
    Considering the complex pathology of neurodegenerative diseases, it is unlikely that drugs that target only one component of cognition, memory, and movement disorders will have satisfactory clinical efficacy. In this study, we have shown that the combination of two identical pharmacophores into a single molecule can unexpectedly result in designed multiple ligands having new inhibitory activities against clinically relevant targets that are not accessible by their monomeric analogues.

On the basis of these results it can be concluded that both ferulic acid- (CAS 537-98-4) and BC-derivs. are not qualified as cognition improving drugs and further studies in this field should be focussed on other pharmaceutical leads to find effective anti-Alzheimer drugs.
